Privacy Policy

Parse – Split the Bill  ·  Last updated: March 2026

Parse is a bill-splitting app that helps groups of people divide a restaurant check. We take your privacy seriously and have designed the app to collect as little data as possible.

Data We Do Not Collect

Parse does not collect, store, or share any data that is linked or linkable to your identity. Specifically, we do not collect:

  • Names, email addresses, or phone numbers
  • Location data
  • Payment or financial information
  • Photos or images
  • Usage analytics or crash reports tied to individuals

Camera Access

Parse requests access to your device's camera solely to photograph restaurant receipts. All image processing (OCR text recognition) is performed entirely on-device using Apple's Vision framework. Receipt images are never uploaded to any server.

Session Data (Real-Time Sync)

When a host creates a bill-splitting session, a temporary session document is written to Google Firebase Firestore. This document contains:

  • The restaurant name from the receipt
  • Itemized prices (no personal financial data)
  • First-name or nickname entered voluntarily by participants
  • Which items each participant claimed

This data is not linked to any account or persistent identity. Sessions are ephemeral and intended only to facilitate real-time coordination between diners. We do not use this data for any other purpose.

Third-Party Services

  • Firebase (Google): Used for real-time session sync. Google's privacy policy applies: policies.google.com/privacy
  • Venmo: Payment requests are initiated via Venmo's deep link. Parse does not handle any payment information. Venmo's privacy policy applies: venmo.com/legal/us-privacy-policy

Data Retention

Session data in Firebase is temporary and not actively retained. We do not maintain databases of user activity or sessions.

Children's Privacy

Parse is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from children.
